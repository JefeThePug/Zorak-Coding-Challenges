Challenge 5

Part One:
Pixel Perfect Panic

You performed so well in the galley that the crew in the generator room has requested your help today. Their Power Harvester machine, which gathers energy from nearby stars to fuel the ship, is functioning fine. However, they are having trouble reading the outputs that indicate how many gigajoules (GJ) of energy have been collected.

Here's how the Power Harvester works:
After sending a photon collection beam toward a star, the energy is gathered and stored in one of the power tanks in the generator room. Although the Power Harvester is somewhat outdated, it has been reliable over the years with minimal updates. Instead of a digital display, this antique machine extrudes a long, thin wire of singular pixels. This wire is then fed into the more modern P.I.L. System (Patterned Image Layout), which reconstructs it into a square image. The resulting image displays the number of GJ harvested.

Despite its age, the Power Harvester is known for its good old-fashioned reliability and continues to run smoothly. However, the P.I.L. System is failing and can no longer convert the wire into a viewable square image. You need to reprogram it to restore this function.

  • You can be certain that the length of wire extruded from the Power Harvester machine is a perfect square, which means that it can be arranged into an image with equal width and height.
  • To create the image, start at point (0,0) and lay wire to the right until you reach the end of the row, indicated by the width of the desired square image. Then, move down a row and lay the wire to the left, zigzagging from top to bottom and left to right until the entire wire is used.

Part Two:
Diamond in the Data Rough

In addition to harvesting gigajoules of power from the stars, the Power Harvester also collects diamond gas as a side effect.

When compressed, this gas forms actual diamonds, which can be soldâ€”likely another reason why this antique machine remains so popular. The diamond gas is filtered into separate containers, and the resulting diamonds help finance the missions of the STS Space Ghost.

Fortunately, the Power Harvester also tracks the amount of diamond gas collected in liters, which is indicated within the extruded wire. Once again, the task of generating an image to display the total liters of diamond gas falls to the P.I.L System, which is still buggy. You'll need to reprogram it to convert the wire into a new formation to read the amount of diamond gas.

  • To create this new image formation, use the same wire. Instead of zigzagging from left to right and top to bottom, zigzag diagonally from the upper left corner (point (0,0)) to the bottom right corner.
  • Start at point (0,0) and lay the wire 1 pixel right, then move diagonally down-left. After completing one diagonal line down-left, lay the wire 1 pixel down and move diagonally up-right. This process continues until the entire wire is used.
  • If the height is reached, the wire will move right instead of down before moving up-right. If the width is reached, the wire will move down instead of right before moving down-left.
