Part One:
Morse De-Code

Welcome to your first day aboard the STS Space Ghost, where you'll be venturing into the vastness of space for the very first time. After settling into your cabin, your next stop is to meet with the Officer on Deck who will provide you with your inaugural job assignment.

To your surprise, you find yourself entrusted with the important task of communications decoding, despite your limited experience in the field. Fortunately, a stroke of luck comes your way when you recall that a significant portion of space communications is conducted using Morse Code.

Although you've heard of Morse Code in passing, your understanding of it is a bit hazy. With a sense of urgency, you frantically search through your desk drawer, and lo and behold! Serendipity leads you to a weathered booklet with yellowed pages with the title "Deciphering Morse Code". Inside, you discover a comprehensive table containing Letter to Morse Code translations, along with a detailed guide on how your machine interprets the input code:

  • Letters in the words will be seperated by spaces
  • Words will be seperated by new lines (\n)
  • Dashes will be represented by underscore characters (_)
  • Dots will be represented by decimal characters (.)
  • Dots and dashes for each letter or character will not have spaces between them

Part Two:
Something Alien...

Just as you translate the question recieved, another source has sent a reply. Perhaps this is the answer to the question previously asked? But the contents of the incoming message cause your script to error. This is not Morse Code. This looks instead like something alien.

Rather than panic and run to your supervisor, you set your mind toward solving this problem on your own. This clearly cannot be the first time your machine has intercepted alien communication. You re-read your Morse Code booklet to no avail and open up your desk drawer to see if there is anything else that might be of use... Nothing.

Sighing heavily and putting your head in your hands, you catch a glimpse of a poster on the wall beside your monitor. It is titled "U.B.M.T. for Alien Communication". Has this been here the whole time? You continue reading...

The poster outlines that before converting from Morse Code to text, alien communication must first be translated into Unicode, and then Binary.

  1. Convert each character to Unicode
  2. Concatenate those numbers to one large integer and convert that number to Binary
  3. Convert the Binary into Morse Code by considering the 0s dots and the 1s dashes
  4. Convert the Morse Code to Text
